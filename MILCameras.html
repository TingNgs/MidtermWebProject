MIL Camera

A mirrorless interchangeable lens camera features a single, removable lens and uses a digital display system rather than an optical viewfinder. The word "mirrorless" indicates that the camera does not have an optical mirror or an optical viewfinder like a conventional Single Lens Reflex camera, but an electronic viewfinder which displays what the camera image sensor sees. Like an SLR, an interchangeable lens mirrorless camera accepts any of a series of interchangeable lenses compatible with the lens mount of that camera. A mirrorless interchangeable lens camera is a further evolution from the film SLR camera, through the digital or DSLR camera, to a mirrorless system camera accepting interchangeable lenses, an MILC.

Pros:

  • Simple
  • Small
  • Light
  • Quite

Cons:

  • Slow Autofocus
  • Short Battery Life

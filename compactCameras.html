Compact Camera

Also known as point-and-shoot camera, is a still camera designed primarily for simple operation. Most use focus free lenses or autofocus for focusing, automatic systems for setting the exposure options, and have flash units built in. Point-and-shoots are by far the best selling type of separate camera, as distinct from camera phones. They are popular for vernacular photography by people who do not consider themselves photographers but want easy-to-use cameras for snapshots of vacations, parties, reunions and other events.

Pros:

  • Lower Cost
  • Easy To Use
  • Portable

Cons:

  • Lower Quality
  • Unable To Upgrade
  • Noise Problem
